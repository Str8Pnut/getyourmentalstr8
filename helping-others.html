How You Can Support Others

Being there for someone in need can make a huge difference. A listening ear, a supportive word, or a simple gesture can go a long way.

Practical Support Tips

  • Check in regularly
  • Encourage professional help if needed
  • Offer to help with daily tasks
  • Respect their privacy and boundaries

Supporting Kids, Teens, and Adults

Kids

Children may not always have the words to express what they’re feeling. Help by creating a safe and supportive environment. Listen to them, maintain routines, use age-appropriate language, and reassure them that it’s okay to talk about feelings.

  • Child Mind Institute
  • KidsHealth

Teens

Teenagers can face overwhelming social, academic, and personal pressures. Let them know you’re available to talk without judgment. Encourage healthy outlets like sports, art, or journaling. Support their independence while also staying involved in their lives.

  • Teen Mental Health
  • Crisis Text Line (Text HOME to 741741)

Adults

Adults often juggle many responsibilities that can lead to stress and burnout. You can help by offering practical support—like babysitting, errands, or just being a consistent friend. Encourage them to take breaks and remind them they don’t have to do everything alone.

  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)
  • Psychology Today - Find a Therapist
