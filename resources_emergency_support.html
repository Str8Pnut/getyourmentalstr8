Emergency Situations

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. Don't hesitate to act — help is available.

If You're in an Abusive Situation

If you’re in an abusive situation and can’t use your phone or internet safely, here are some tips to help you protect yourself and seek support:

  • Have a safety plan: Think through where you could go and how to get there in an emergency.
  • Use code words: Set up code words with trusted friends or family to signal when you need help.
  • Hide essentials: Keep a small emergency bag hidden with ID, cash, clothes, and important items.
  • Visit public places: Libraries, clinics, or community centers often have phones or internet access.
  • Connect quietly: If it's safe, talk to a trusted neighbor, teacher, or coworker who may be able to help or contact services on your behalf.

For confidential help, you can also contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 800-799-7233 when safe to do so.
